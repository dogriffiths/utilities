Working with Images Using Generative AI

Summary from the Image Bootcamp

 

Key Principles

  • AI images can look GENERIC and reduce apparent quality
  • Network services can be SLOW or UNSTABLE
  • Chatbots are NOT DETERMINISTIC - same input = different outputs
  • Be careful with PERSONAL INFORMATION (apply the "New York Times rule")
  • There is no PERFECT SERVICE - different strengths, weaknesses, and costs
  • Services are IMPROVING RAPIDLY - what's true today may change soon

The AI Image Problem

Why AI-generated images can backfire

  • Easy to recognize: AI images have a distinctive look that people can spot
  • Quality perception: Using too many AI images can reduce the perceived value of your work
  • Getting better: Errors like weird hands and impossible poses are becoming less common
  • Challenge: How to make AI images look less generic and more professional

Making Images Less Generic

Techniques to improve AI image quality

  • Style specification: Request specific artistic styles (watercolor, oil painting, photorealistic)
  • Reference images: Provide examples of the look you want to achieve
  • Detailed prompts: Be specific about lighting, composition, color palette, and mood
  • Iterate and refine: Don't accept the first result - ask for variations and improvements

Images as Communication

Using images to interact with AI

  • Upload and analyze: AI can read text from images, identify objects, and understand context
  • Visual prompts: Show the AI what you want instead of describing it
  • Image-to-image: Transform existing images into new styles or formats
  • Multimodal interaction: Combine images with text for more precise instructions

Analyzing and Processing Images

What AI can do with images

  • Content extraction: Pull text, data, and information from images
  • Object recognition: Identify items, people (with privacy considerations), and scenes
  • Style analysis: Understand artistic techniques and visual elements
  • Conversion: Transform sketches into photorealistic images or vice versa

AI Video Generation

Creating videos with generative AI

  • Text-to-video: Generate video clips from text descriptions
  • Transition clips: Create smooth transitions between two frames
  • Storyboarding: Convert sketches into realistic previsualization
  • Cost consideration: Premium services can be expensive ($20+ per clip)

Video Workflow Process

Building AI-generated videos efficiently

  1. Storyboard: Create sketches of key scenes
  2. Generate keyframes: Convert sketches to photorealistic images
  3. Source basic clips: Film or generate your main footage
  4. Create transition clips: Use AI to join scenes smoothly
  5. Edit together: Use video editing software to assemble the final product

Key principle: Do work as early as possible - it's cheaper to refine in the storyboard phase than in production

Transition Clips

Bridging scenes with AI

  • How it works: Provide a start frame and end frame, AI generates the motion between them
  • Use cases: Cinematic transitions, scene changes (jungle to desert), character movement
  • Services: Google Veo (premium), Kling AI (mid-tier)
  • Cost example: A one-minute storyboarded video can cost $80+ to produce

Pre-visualization Benefits

Why AI video is valuable for production

  • Cost savings: Expensive for casual use, cheap compared to traditional film production
  • Better planning: Transform simple storyboards into realistic visual representations
  • Client communication: Show stakeholders what the final product will look like
  • Faster iteration: Test different approaches before committing to expensive shoots

AI Image Services

Cloud-based options

  • ChatGPT: General purpose, good for quick generation
  • Google Gemini: Integrated with Google services
  • Meta AI: Improving rapidly, still developing
  • Mid Journey: Famous for artistic illustrations
  • Adobe Firefly: Focused on graphic designers
  • Stable Diffusion: Can be self-hosted (requires technical skill)

AI Video Services

Options for different budgets

  • Budget/Mid-tier: LumaLabs (based on footage), HeyGen (AI dubbing), Kling AI (start/end frames)
  • Premium: Google Veo (most practical), Sora (recently improved), Runway ML (high-end)
  • Self-hosted: Requires powerful machine, lots of time, and technical expertise
  • Limitations: Services like Veo allow only 2-3 free videos per day

Important Warnings

What to watch out for

  • Privacy: Don't share sensitive information you wouldn't want on the front page of newspapers
  • Costs: Premium video services can be very expensive ($20-80+ per short video)
  • Reliability: Services can be slow or go down during live use
  • Variability: Same prompt will produce different results each time
  • Ethics: Some services now allow generating videos with public figures

Best Practices

Getting the most from AI image and video tools

  • Experiment: Try different services - each has unique strengths
  • Stay updated: Services improve rapidly; what's bad today may be good tomorrow
  • Plan ahead: For video work, do as much as possible in pre-production
  • Verify outputs: Always check AI-generated content for accuracy and appropriateness
  • Consider costs: Budget carefully if using premium video services
